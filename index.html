About

Who I Am

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Hanyang University, specializing in vehicle energy management strategy (EMS) optimization for advanced electrified powertrains. My research bridges classical optimal control — Dynamic Programming (DP) and Pontryagin's Minimum Principle (PMP/ECMS) — with modern machine learning and reinforcement learning approaches.

Currently based at Argonne National Laboratory as a Research Aide, I contribute to NHTSA-funded vehicle technology assessment. My work spans the full development pipeline: reverse-engineering real-world test data, building physics-based powertrain models, deriving optimal control strategies, and validating against standardized drive cycles.
