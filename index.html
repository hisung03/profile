About

Who I Am

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Hanyang University, South Korea, specializing in vehicle energy management strategy (EMS) optimization and intelligent control for advanced powertrain systems. My research bridges classical optimal control theory — Dynamic Programming (DP) and Pontryagin's Minimum Principle (PMP) — with modern machine learning and reinforcement learning approaches.

Currently, I am also serving as a Research Aide at Argonne National Laboratory, contributing to NHTSA-funded vehicle technology assessment and model development projects. I am passionate about accelerating the electrification of transportation through rigorous mathematical modeling, simulation, and data-driven control strategies.
